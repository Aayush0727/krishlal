Cultivation is essential because natural elements often create a hard, crusty layer on the soil surface, which can impede the movement of air, nutrients, and water. By breaking up this crusty layer, cultivation allows these vital resources to penetrate deeper into the soil, making them more accessible to plant roots and promoting healthier plant growth.

A fertilizer is any substance, either natural or synthetic, that is added to soil or plant tissues to provide essential nutrients needed for plant growth. Fertilizers help enhance soil fertility and promote healthy plant development. They come in various forms, including natural options like compost and manure, as well as industrially produced products.

Harvesting is the process of collecting mature crops from the fields once they are ready for harvest. This involves cutting and gathering crops like grains or pulses. Traditionally, harvesting was done using hand tools such as a scythe or sickle. On smaller farms with limited machinery, harvesting remains one of the most labor-intensive tasks of the growing season.

Sowing is the process of planting seeds in the soil to grow crops or plants. It involves placing seeds in the ground or a growing medium, where they will germinate and develop into plants. An area where seeds have been planted is referred to as having been "sowed.

Irrigation is the process of providing controlled amounts of water to plants at specific intervals. This practice is essential for growing agricultural crops, maintaining landscapes, and restoring vegetation in dry areas or during periods of below-average rainfall. It ensures that plants receive the necessary moisture to thrive, even when natural precipitation is insufficient.
